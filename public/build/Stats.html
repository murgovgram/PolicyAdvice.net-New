Vision insurance is the type of insurance that covers routine eye health expenses. It may also reduce out-of-pocket costs for eye exams. However, even if your eyesight is perfect, regular eye exams are essential to check if everything is fine.

Vision insurance can help you do that in a cost-effective way. Insurance companies sell different types of vision insurance coverage. Usually, they include standard, basic, enhanced, and advanced levels. Therefore, the premium fee for vision insurance varies from level to level. Finding the best vision insurance company can be difficult, considering the number of companies operating in the US.

There are two types of vision insurance plans: vision benefit plans and discount vision plans.

With vision benefits plans, you pay a monthly premium fee and get coverage for annual eye exams, lenses, eyewear, and other eye care needs.

On the other hand, discount vision plans are somewhat similar to vision benefit plans as they cover the same types of eye exams, frames, and lenses. However, the only difference between the two is that the fee for the discount vision plan is significantly lower than the monthly premium for the vision benefit plan. With discount vision plans, you can expect to get a discount of 15% to 35%.
